Product Discovery

MoSCoW method

The product discovery journey began with an extensive benchmarking analysis of various browser gaming services currently accessible on the internet. A thorough assessment of both aesthetic and functional elements was conducted to inform the integration of features into a structured framework using the MoSCoW Method.

Naming

A naming dynamic was created involving professionals from the UX, Marketing, Dev, and Product departments. The purpose of this activity was to score a series of names in order to find the best among three pillars: 1 - Fun; 2 - Identification; and 3 - Video game. In the end, the winner was: Gamezilla!

Website MVP

To identify if browser gaming was profitable via ads monetization, we launched a website MVP using NextUI Kit. This co-creation between design and development allowed the team to adhere to the “fail fast” philosophy.

Responsive Design

During the hand-off process, we chose to design for six different screen types and resolutions: A - Desktop Large (1920x1080 px); B - Desktop (1440x900 px); C - Tablet (1024x768 px); D - Mobile (768x1024 px); E - Android (412x915 px); and F - Android Small (360x640 px).

The combination of each screen type with its resolution was meticulously designed to accommodate the elements in the interface as effectively as possible. Thus, it was possible to make the Gamezilla website completely responsive.
